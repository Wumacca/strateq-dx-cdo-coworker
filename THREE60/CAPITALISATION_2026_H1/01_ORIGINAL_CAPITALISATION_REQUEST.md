# THREE60 Operations Digital Capitalisation Programme  
## Original Capitalisation Request Summary

**Programme period:** January to July 2026  
**Approved capital value:** £229,602.18  
**Workstream:** Operations Digital Transformation  
**Purpose:** To define what the approved capitalisation programme set out to do.

---

## 1. Executive Intent

The original capitalisation request sought approval to execute the Operations digital maturity roadmap and move THREE60 from low digital maturity toward a controlled, scalable operating model.

The operating model itself was positioned as a strategic asset.

This was not a request to fund isolated software tools. It was a request to capitalise the Digital Transformation Team and the supporting functional input required to design, govern, prioritise, and support the digital maturity roadmap.

---

## 2. Scope Clarification

The approved capitalisation covered:

- Digital Transformation Team effort
- supporting functional input
- initiative shaping
- governance
- prioritisation
- delivery support
- reporting
- maturity roadmap execution
- benefits enablement

The approved capitalisation did not automatically include:

- software licences
- external development support
- KL development resources
- fully scoped implementation costs for each initiative

Individual initiatives were to be scoped, costed, and approved separately through CTR where required.

---

## 3. Original Risk of Inaction

The original case identified the following enterprise risks:

| Risk Area | Original Risk |
|---|---|
| Profit | Continued margin dilution from manual processes, duplicated effort, and rework. |
| Risk / Assurance | Increased delivery, audit, reputational, and contract exposure. |
| Capacity / Revenue | Constrained capacity to deliver and take on additional work. |
| Governance | Continued reliance on reactive intervention rather than systemised digital controls. |
| Visibility | Delayed or inconsistent cost, delivery, KPI, and assurance visibility. |

---

## 4. Original Benefit Intent

| Benefit Area | Original Intent | Closeout Measurement Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Profit | Enable 0.5%–1.5% EBITDA margin improvement through improved execution control and visibility. | Treat as value enabled, not fully realised, unless post-adoption financial evidence exists. |
| Risk | Improve confidence in delivery of secured and future Operations contracts through stronger digital control and assurance. | Evidence through Reg 5, KPI visibility, adoption gates, action governance, COSHH control, and reporting improvements. |
| Revenue / Capacity | Increase executable delivery capacity and selective growth without proportional overhead increase. | Evidence through workflow digitisation, automation, pipeline governance, and reduced manual coordination burden. |

## 7. Original Ownership Model

The original capitalisation request established a clear ownership model:

| Role | Ownership |
|---|---|
| Leadership Team | Business outcomes, strategic priorities, investment criteria, funding approval, benefits accountability. |
| Digital Transformation Team | Digital maturity roadmap delivery, enterprise visibility layers, workflow automation, action management, standards, controls, governance, sequencing, and reporting. |

---

## 8. Closeout Test

The closeout report must answer five questions:

1. Did the programme move THREE60 toward a controlled, scalable digital operating model?
2. Did the programme deliver the approved roadmap scope or control any variance?
3. Did the programme strengthen risk, assurance, reporting, and Duty Holder control?
4. Did the programme establish a repeatable governance model?
5. Did the programme create a justified basis for the next six-month capitalisation request?
