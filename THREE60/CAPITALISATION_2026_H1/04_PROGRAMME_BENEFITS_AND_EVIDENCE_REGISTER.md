# THREE60 Operations Digital Capitalisation Programme

## Programme Benefits and Evidence Register

**Programme period:** January to July 2026  
**Workstream:** Operations Digital Transformation  
**Document status:** Working baseline for Board closeout and next capitalisation request  
**Document owner:** Digital Transformation Team  
**Purpose:** To hold the controlled evidence base for what the capitalisation programme set out to deliver, what was delivered, what benefits were enabled, and what evidence supports Board-level reporting.

---

## 1. Executive Control Position

The January to July 2026 capitalisation programme was approved to move the Operations workstream from low digital maturity toward a controlled, scalable operating model.

The programme has delivered against that intent.

The evidence demonstrates that the Digital Transformation Team has established the enterprise control layers required to convert individual digital initiatives into a repeatable digital operating model. The programme has delivered core roadmap initiatives, absorbed additional emergent work without additional capital cost, strengthened Duty Holder assurance reporting, and established the governance process required for the next phase of digital transformation.

The closeout position is:

- Approved capitalisation initiatives have been delivered or are in final controlled rollout.
- Delivery remains under budget at programme level, subject to final finance reconciliation.
- Emergent work has been absorbed within the programme envelope without additional capital request.
- KPI reporting was presented at Reg 5 with no observations raised.
- COSHH 365 and Omega Incident Management have passed post-adoption gates.
- Digital governance has moved from informal initiative handling to a governed intake, hopper, delivery, closeout, and adoption model.
- The next six-month request should be positioned as the continuation and scaling of an enterprise digital management system, not a new set of isolated tool deployments.

Evidence in this register is classified as follows:

| Evidence Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|

| Supported | Evidence is supported by programme communications or live delivery status but requires final closeout pack confirmation. |
| Pending | Evidence requires finance extract, adoption data, stakeholder sign-off, or formal closeout evidence before Board claim. |

## 6. Board-Safe Benefit Statements

### 6.1 Enterprise Risk and Assurance

The programme has strengthened enterprise risk and assurance control by moving critical reporting, action management, COSHH management, and incident investigation workflows into governed digital routes. Reg 5 KPI reporting was delivered with no observations raised, and COSHH 365 and Omega Incident Management have passed adoption gates.

### 6.2 Duty Holder / Regulatory Expectation

The programme has improved THREE60’s ability to evidence control in a Duty Holder context, as demonstrated during the recent Reg 5 BP audit. COSHH 365 removes reliance on client systems for chemical assessment management, KPI reporting strengthens enterprise visibility, and Omega 365 establishes a stronger enterprise action governance route.

### 6.3 Operating Model and Governance

The programme has established the operating model required for repeatable digital transformation. Intake, hopper prioritisation, initiative governance, delivery tracking, adoption gates, and benefits evidence are now part of a controlled digital management process.

### 6.4 Scalability and Sustainability

The programme has moved digital delivery away from isolated tool implementation and toward a scalable enterprise model. The same governance structure is now being applied across KPI reporting, action management, COSHH, incident investigation, Chronos developments, CMMS selection, Training & Competence, and transition support.

### 6.5 Efficiency, Automation, and Tooling

The programme has delivered practical workflow improvements across KPI automation, Chronos development, action management, and COSHH management. Automation is complete for immediate KPI system integrations, with follow-on systems to be connected as the long-term system landscape is established.

### 6.6 Digital Maturity Movement

The closeout pack must include an updated Digital Maturity Register showing movement from the original maturity baseline to the current post-programme position. This is the evidence that the programme delivered operating model maturity, not only initiative activity.
