# 05_DIGITAL_GOVERNANCE_PROCESS.md

# THREE60 Operations Digital Capitalisation Programme  
## Digital Governance Process

**Purpose:** To describe the digital governance process established during the first six-month capitalisation programme and provide the basis for Board closeout slides.

---

## 1. Executive Governance Position

The programme has established a repeatable digital governance process for the Operations workstream.

The process moves digital improvement away from informal requests, isolated tool decisions, and reactive issue handling. It creates a controlled route from business need through intake, hopper prioritisation, scope definition, approval, delivery, adoption, and closeout.

This is now a core part of the enterprise digital operating model.

---

## 2. Governance Model

The governed lifecycle is:

```text
Business Need / Opportunity
        ↓
Digital Intake
        ↓
Initiative Hopper
        ↓
Prioritisation and Route Decision
        ↓
Initiation Form / Charter / CTR
        ↓
Delivery Governance
        ↓
Testing / Acceptance / Adoption
        ↓
Closeout / Benefits Evidence
        ↓
BAU Ownership / Continuous Improvement
```

---

## 3. Stage Definitions

| Stage | Purpose | Control Output |
|---|---|---|
| Business Need / Opportunity | Capture a pain point, risk, manual process, reporting gap, assurance issue, or improvement opportunity. | Initial business reason. |
| Digital Intake | Standardise the request and capture minimum information. | Intake record. |
| Initiative Hopper | Hold all candidate initiatives in one controlled view for prioritisation. | Hopper / initiative register. |
| Prioritisation and Route Decision | Determine urgency, impact, feasibility, risk, dependency, and appropriate route. | Priority position and route decision. |
| Initiation Form / Charter / CTR | Define scope, benefits, ownership, cost route, approvals, and system boundaries. | Approved initiation form / charter / CTR. |
| Delivery Governance | Control scope, schedule, risks, dependencies, costs, and adoption readiness. | Delivery tracker and status reporting. |
| Testing / Acceptance / Adoption | Confirm the solution is fit for use and accepted by the business. | UAT evidence, adoption gate, training evidence. |
| Closeout / Benefits Evidence | Record delivery outcome, variance, adoption status, benefits enabled, and open actions. | Closeout record and benefits evidence update. |
| BAU Ownership / Continuous Improvement | Transfer to business ownership and keep improvement loop active. | BAU owner, support route, future hopper items. |

---

## 4. Governance Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Enterprise control before tool deployment | No initiative proceeds as a standalone tool decision without ownership, scope, value, governance, and system impact defined. |
| One governed hopper | The hopper is the controlled portfolio of digital demand and future improvement work. |
| Functions own business outcomes | Functional teams define requirements, adopt tools, and own operational outcomes. |
| Digital owns the enterprise layers | Digital owns governance, architecture, delivery control, data/reporting standards, and system integration logic. |
| CTR before live delivery | Approved initiatives must pass the required commercial and governance route before execution commitment. |
| Adoption is part of delivery | Delivery is not complete until acceptance, adoption, and ownership are evidenced. |
| Benefits must be evidenced | Claims are recorded in the benefits register and updated through post-adoption review. |
| Emergent work is controlled | Urgent or additional scope can be absorbed only when explicitly captured, prioritised, and governed. |

## 5. Board Slide Narrative

### Slide Message 1 — Governance Established

The first capitalisation programme has established the digital governance process required to move from informal requests to controlled digital delivery.

The business now has a repeatable route for digital demand: intake, hopper, prioritisation, approval, delivery, adoption, closeout, and BAU ownership.

### Slide Message 2 — Why It Matters

The governance process protects value by ensuring that digital initiatives are selected, sequenced, approved, delivered, and adopted through a controlled operating model.

This prevents isolated tool decisions, duplicate spend, unclear ownership, weak adoption, and uncontrolled scope growth.

### Slide Message 3 — What It Enables Next

The next six-month capitalisation request can now be governed through a live hopper and evidence-based priority view. This allows the Board to approve the next programme of work against a clear portfolio, rather than reviewing disconnected requests.
