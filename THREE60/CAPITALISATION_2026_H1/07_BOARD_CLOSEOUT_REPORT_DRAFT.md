## 1. Executive Summary

The first six-month Operations Digital Capitalisation Programme was approved to move THREE60 from low digital maturity toward a controlled, scalable operating model.

The programme has delivered against that intent.

The programme has delivered the core digital maturity roadmap, absorbed additional emergent operational priorities without requesting additional capital funding, strengthened Duty Holder assurance reporting, and established the governance process required to manage digital transformation as a repeatable enterprise capability.

Delivery remains under budget at programme level, subject to final finance evidence. The majority of approved initiatives are complete, live, adopted, or in final controlled rollout. Where initiatives have moved beyond the original programme window, the variance is controlled and linked to higher-priority operational constraints, not unmanaged delivery failure.

The next capitalisation request should therefore be positioned as the continuation and scaling of the enterprise digital management system now established.

## 2. What the Programme Set Out to Do

The original capitalisation request sought approval to execute the Operations digital maturity roadmap for a capital cost of £229,602.18.

The stated intent was to move the Operations workstream from low digital maturity to a controlled, scalable operating model.

The capitalisation covered the Digital Transformation Team and supporting functional input required to:

- govern the roadmap;
- shape and prioritise initiatives;
- support delivery;
- define enterprise standards;
- improve visibility and reporting;
- strengthen action management;
- improve workflow digitisation;
- improve assurance control;
- establish the digital governance process.

The capitalisation did not automatically include all software licences, external development, or implementation costs. Those were to be handled through separate CTR / initiative approval routes where required.

## 7. Digital Governance Process Established

The programme established a governed route for digital demand and delivery:

```text
Business Need / Opportunity
        ↓
Digital Intake
        ↓
Initiative Hopper
        ↓
Prioritisation and Route Decision
        ↓
Initiation Form / Charter / CTR
        ↓
Delivery Governance
        ↓
Testing / Acceptance / Adoption
        ↓
Closeout / Benefits Evidence
        ↓
BAU Ownership / Continuous Improvement
```

This process is the enterprise control layer that converts tools into governed value.

It ensures that:

- requests are captured consistently;
- opportunities are held in a controlled hopper;
- priorities are visible to leadership;
- scope is defined before delivery;
- approvals are recorded;
- delivery is tracked;
- adoption is evidenced;
- benefits are recorded;
- future demand can be sequenced through a single digital roadmap.
