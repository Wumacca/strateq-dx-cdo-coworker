# THREE60 Operations Digital Capitalisation Programme  
## Digital Maturity Register

**Programme period:** January to July 2026  
**Document status:** Working baseline — must be validated before Board closeout  
**Purpose:** To demonstrate movement from the original maturity baseline to the post-programme position.

---

## 1. Executive Position

The original capitalisation request was justified by a maturity constraint.

The closeout pack must therefore demonstrate maturity movement, not only initiative delivery.

This register records the baseline maturity position, the target set by the capitalisation request, the current post-programme position, and the evidence required to support any claimed movement.

No maturity movement should be claimed as final until the scoring has been reviewed and approved against evidence.

---

## 2. Maturity Scoring Control

| Score | Maturity Level | Definition |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Initial | Activity is fragmented, reactive, spreadsheet-led, person-dependent, or not consistently governed. |
| 2 | Developing | Some tools, processes, or governance are in place, but control is inconsistent or not fully integrated. |
| 3 | Controlled / Mature | Process, ownership, governance, reporting, and adoption are defined, repeatable, and evidenced. |
| 4 | Leading | Integrated, optimised, measured, scalable, and continuously improved across the enterprise. |

## 4. Board-Safe Maturity Statement

The programme has created demonstrable maturity movement across the domains targeted by the original capitalisation request.

The strongest evidenced movement is in:

- Data & Visibility;
- Governance & Change;
- User Tools;
- Action Management.

Process Digitisation has moved forward through Chronos workflow delivery, COSHH, Omega, and Salus implementation progress, but final maturity scoring should remain controlled until Salus rollout and remaining department-dependent Chronos items are complete.

Digital Skills was intentionally deprioritised in the original capitalisation programme and should be reported as stable unless separate evidence is added.

## 7. Current Scoring Caveat

The proposed current scores are working positions for evidence collation.

They should be reviewed before Board issue.

The Board deck should avoid presenting decimal maturity scores unless the scoring method is accepted. A safer slide treatment is:

```text
Baseline: Low / developing maturity
Current: controlled foundation established
Next target: scale and harden maturity across priority domains
```

For the Board closeout, the strongest visual is a maturity movement table using arrows and evidence statements rather than over-precise numerical claims.
